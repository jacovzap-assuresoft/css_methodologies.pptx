Philosophy

OOCSS SMACSS BEM
Encourages the creation of reusable and modular CSS components by separating structure and skin. Emphasizes the categorization of CSS rules into five categories to improve maintainability and scalability. Focuses on creating independent and reusable blocks, elements, and modifiers to enhance code modularity.

Flexibility

OOCSS SMACSS BEM
Provides flexibility by allowing developers to reuse styles across different components. Offers flexibility in terms of how categories are applied, allowing developers to adapt the methodology to their project's needs. Ensures flexibility through independent and encapsulated blocks, making it easy to modify and extend styles without affecting other components.

Maintenance

OOCSS SMACSS BEM
Can lead to increased maintenance if not managed properly due to the generic nature of classes. Aids in maintainability by providing a clear structure and organization of styles. Requires adherence to the methodology for optimal results. Promotes easy maintenance by creating self-contained and isolated components, reducing the risk of style conflicts.

Learning Curve

OOCSS SMACSS BEM
Has a relatively low learning curve, as it focuses on separating structure and skin. Moderate learning curve, as developers need to understand and adhere to the five categories of styles. Moderate learning curve, especially for understanding the naming conventions and the relationship between Blocks, Elements, and Modifiers.

Use Cases

OOCSS SMACSS BEM
Suitable for projects with a need for a balance between reusability and simplicity. Ideal for larger projects where a clear and scalable structure is crucial. Well-suited for modular and scalable designs, especially in large and complex web applications.
